Before You Start

Essential Tips for Accurate Measurements

  • Use a soft measuring tape - Fabric measuring tapes are more accurate than rigid rulers
  • Wear fitted clothing - Measure over undergarments or very fitted clothes
  • Stand naturally - Don't suck in your stomach or puff out your chest
  • Get help - Ask someone to help you measure, especially for back measurements
  • Measure twice - Double-check all measurements for accuracy
  • Keep the tape level - Make sure the measuring tape is parallel to the floor
Important: All measurements should be taken in inches. If you only have a centimeter tape, divide by 2.54 to convert to inches.

Male Measurements

1. Neck

How to measure: Wrap the measuring tape around the base of your neck where a shirt collar would naturally sit. Keep one finger between the tape and your neck for comfort.

Typical range: 14" - 18"

2. Shoulder

How to measure: Measure from the edge of one shoulder (where the arm meets the shoulder) straight across to the edge of the other shoulder across your back.

Typical range: 16" - 20"

3. Chest

How to measure: Wrap the tape around the fullest part of your chest, usually just under your armpits. Keep the tape level and snug but not tight.

Typical range: 36" - 50"

4. Waist

How to measure: Measure around your natural waistline, which is usually the narrowest part of your torso, just above your hip bones.

Typical range: 30" - 46"

5. Hip

How to measure: Measure around the fullest part of your hips, usually about 7-9 inches below your waist.

Typical range: 36" - 50"

6. Sleeve Length

How to measure: With your arm slightly bent, measure from the center of the back of your neck, over your shoulder, down your arm to your wrist.

Typical range: 32" - 36"

7. Shirt Length

How to measure: Measure from the base of your neck (where it meets your shoulder) straight down to where you want the shirt to end.

Typical range: 28" - 32"

8. Pant Length

How to measure: Measure from your waist down to where you want the pants to end (usually at your ankle or shoe).

Typical range: 40" - 46"

9. Inseam

How to measure: Measure from your crotch down to your ankle along the inside of your leg. This is best done with help.

Typical range: 28" - 36"

10. Thigh

How to measure: Measure around the fullest part of your thigh, usually about 2-3 inches below your crotch.

Typical range: 20" - 28"

11. Calf

How to measure: Measure around the fullest part of your calf muscle.

Typical range: 14" - 18"

Female Measurements

1. Shoulder

How to measure: Measure from the edge of one shoulder straight across to the edge of the other shoulder across your back.

Typical range: 14" - 17"

2. Bust

How to measure: Wrap the tape around the fullest part of your bust, usually across your nipples. Keep the tape level and snug but not tight.

Typical range: 32" - 44"

3. Waist

How to measure: Measure around your natural waistline, which is usually the narrowest part of your torso.

Typical range: 24" - 38"

4. Hip

How to measure: Measure around the fullest part of your hips, usually about 7-9 inches below your waist.

Typical range: 34" - 46"

5. Sleeve Length

How to measure: With your arm slightly bent, measure from your shoulder point down to your wrist.

Typical range: 22" - 25"

6. Armhole

How to measure: Measure around your armpit area where the sleeve would attach to the garment.

Typical range: 16" - 20"

7. Blouse Length

How to measure: Measure from your shoulder point straight down to where you want the blouse to end.

Typical range: 22" - 26"

8. Dress Length

How to measure: Measure from your shoulder point straight down to where you want the dress to end.

Typical range: 36" - 44"

9. Skirt Length

How to measure: Measure from your waist down to where you want the skirt to end.

Typical range: 18" - 30"

10. Around Neck

How to measure: Measure around the base of your neck where a necklace would naturally sit.

Typical range: 12" - 15"

11. Around Upper Arm

How to measure: Measure around the fullest part of your upper arm, usually about 2 inches below your armpit.

Typical range: 10" - 14"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't measure over bulky clothing - This will give inaccurate measurements
  • Don't pull the tape too tight - The tape should be snug but not constricting
  • Don't measure when you're bloated - Take measurements when you feel normal
  • Don't round numbers - Be as precise as possible, use quarter inches
  • Don't skip measurements - Even if you think you know your size, measure everything
